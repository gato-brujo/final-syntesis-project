Red and Blue-shift

In physics and astronomy, redshift is a phenomenon that occurs when electromagnetic radiation that is emitted or reflected from an object, usually visible light, appears redshifted at the end of the electromagnetic spectrum. When we speak of redshift, what we are indicating is that the waves that are perceived are a little closer to the type of waves of greater amplitude (radio waves), while when we speak of blue shift we are referring to the opposite effect, i.e., we are closer to the waves of shorter length.

Red-shift

Occurs when an electromagnetic wave (usually visible light), which is emitted from an object, is redshifted at the end of the electromagnetic spectrum. It should be noted that this redshift is an apparent increase in wavelength.
